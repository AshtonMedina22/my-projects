Best Practices in AI Deployment
Data Hygiene & Privacy Basics
38 min
Before our AI chatbot can assist customers, we need to make sure it handles their data responsibly. Customer conversations can contain sensitive personal information, including email addresses, phone numbers, account details, and sometimes even payment information. If this data isn’t protected properly, we could expose customers to identity theft, violate privacy laws, and destroy trust.

Why Data Protection Matters
Imagine a customer contacts our chatbot saying: “I can’t log into my account. My email is sarah.johnson@email.com, and I’m trying to use my credit card ending in 4532.” This conversation gets stored in our system for training and improving the AI model. Without proper data safeguards in place:

Sarah’s email could be leaked in a data breach, leading to spam or phishing attacks
Her payment details might be exposed to unauthorized employees or contractors
The AI model might accidentally repeat Sarah’s information to other customers
The company could face legal penalties for mishandling personal data
Customers lose trust when they discover their private information wasn’t protected
Data Safeguard Steps
1. Redact Sensitive Information

Personally Identifiable Information (PII) is any data that could identify a specific person. Our chatbot logs shouldn’t store this information in plain 
[text](https://www.codecademy.com/resources/docs/ai/generative-ai/text)

. Instead, we’ll detect and replace it with placeholders:

Email addresses: sarah.johnson@email.com → [EMAIL]
Phone numbers: 555-123-4567 → [PHONE]
Credit card numbers: 1234-5678-9012-3456 → [CREDIT_CARD]
Social Security Numbers: 123-45-6789 → [SSN]
When engineers review conversations to improve the AI, they see: “I can’t log into my account. My email is [EMAIL] and I’m trying to use my card ending in [CREDIT_CARD].” They can still understand the conversation context without accessing sensitive data. For standard numerical formats, often regular expressions can be used to identify such content, which you will try in the checkpoints.

2. Remove Identity Traces

Even without obvious PII, data can still be used to identify individuals. Anonymization adds extra protection, for example:

Hash customer IDs: Transform “customer_12345” into “a3f8d9e1b2c4” (one-way conversion that can’t be reversed)
Remove names: Strip out any customer names from the conversation
Convert absolute to relative time: Instead of “2025-01-15 3:42 PM,” use “3 days ago”.
3. Apply Differential Privacy to Statistics

Imagine we’re analyzing which ZIP codes have the most billing issues and publish this report:

ZIP Code    Billing Issues
94102       247
94103       189
94104       1

The same customer always gets the same hash (you can still track their conversations)
Different customers get different hashes
Nobody can figure out the original customer ID from the hash
